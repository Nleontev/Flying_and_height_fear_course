The main three methods we use

Cognitive Restructuring

Awareness: Recognizing and becoming aware of the negative thought patterns and cognitive distortions that contribute to distress. Challenging: Questioning the negative thoughts and providing evidence against them to create a more balanced perspective. Reframing: Creating alternative, more rational, and constructive thoughts that replace the negative ones.

Deep breathing involves taking slow, deep breaths to activate the diaphragm, the muscle beneath the lungs. PMR helps release physical tension from the body. It involves systematically tensing and relaxing different muscle groups. Mindfulness meditation involves being fully present in the moment, observing your thoughts and sensations without judgment

Exposure Therapy
- Virtual Reality (VR) Sessions

The therapist evaluates the individual's anxiety triggers and creates a personalized virtual environment that reflects those triggers. The individual wears a VR headset and enters the computer-generated environment, which can be a simulated public space, heights, flying, combat scenarios, or any other relevant situation.
